Hyperloop Explained

Hyperloop is the first new major mode of transportation in 100 years. It's designed to be safe, energy efficient, and reliable. It will take you directly to your destination at speeds of up to 700mph, above land or underground.

How it became a reality?

We have developed a proprietary electric propulsion system which was tested first in May 2016 in an open-air propulsion test, in May 2017 in our first full-systems test, and again in July 2017 during our Phase 2 testing.

Why Do We Need A Hyperloop?

A global economy requires faster, cheaper and more efficient transportation models. Our roads, airports and ports are congested. We haven't had a major new form of transportation in 100 years, and we're due for one, especially one that is ultra-fast, on-demand, direct, emmission-free, energy efficient, and has a smaller footprint than other high-speed transport models.
